Limitless Canvas

Interactive Creative Tool for Children with Physical Limitations. innovative project designed for Ali'an Hospital Rehabilitation Center for Children and Youth. It addresses the creative needs of children with physical limitations by providing an adaptive tool that allows them to create intricate digital art without conventional physical interaction. this project enables children to explore their artistic potential and engage in creative expression with minimal physical effort.
The project features a height-adjustable pendulum, allowing users to create intricate designs on a screen by pushing and moving the ball. By harnessing the ball’s inertia, even a small push can generate complex and layered results.
Attributes
01
Movement Translation
The project features a 3D-printed ball embedded with a motion sensor and an Arduino at the pendulum's base. When the child taps or moves the ball, the pendulum's motion is transformed into intricate shapes and patterns on a digital screen. The sensors capture the pendulum's movement, converting it into visual designs, allowing disabled children to create complex artwork with minimal physical effort. The artwork is generated using Processing.
02
Adaptive Interface
The interface is intuitive and user-friendly, offering real-time feedback and guiding the child through the creative process. It responds precisely to the pendulum’s movement, ensuring a seamless translation from physical gestures to digital art.
